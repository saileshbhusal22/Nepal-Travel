✦ KATHMANDU VALLEY • NEPAL ✦

The City Where
Gods Still Walk

A living fusion of Hindu and Buddhist heritage, ancient Newari architecture, chaotic alleys, divine temples, and the warmest smiles in the Himalayas.

Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is far more than just a gateway to the Himalayas. Nestled in the Kathmandu Valley at about 1,400 meters, this ancient city is a vibrant mosaic of over 2,000 years of history, art, spirituality, and resilient culture. Home to seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites, it blends chaotic energy with profound peace — from the prayer wheels of Boudhanath to the sacred flames of Pashupatinath.

A Living History

The Kathmandu Valley was once a vast lake, according to legend. The bodhisattva Manjushri drained it with his sword, creating the fertile land we see today.

The city itself dates back to the Licchavi period (400–750 AD), flourishing under the Malla kings (12th–18th century) who built the magnificent Durbar Squares. In 1769, Prithvi Narayan Shah unified Nepal and made Kathmandu the capital.

Today, despite the 2015 earthquake and ongoing challenges, the valley remains a testament to Newari craftsmanship, with intricately carved wooden windows, pagoda-style temples, and a deep syncretic tradition where Hindus and Buddhists worship side by side.

Iconic Places to Explore

UNESCO SITE • MONKEY TEMPLE

Swayambhunath Stupa

Perched on a hill west of Kathmandu, this ancient Buddhist stupa (dating back to the 5th century) offers panoramic views of the valley. Known for its iconic Buddha eyes and resident monkeys, it symbolizes self-existent enlightenment.

Climb 365 steps at sunrise for the best experience.

LARGEST STUPA IN NEPAL

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest spherical stupas in the world, surrounded by Tibetan monasteries and prayer flags. Walk the kora (circumambulation) with monks and locals — a deeply meditative experience.

HINDU PILGRIMAGE SITE

Pashupatinath Temple

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this sacred complex on the banks of the Bagmati River is one of the most important Hindu temples in South Asia. Witness the evening aarti for an unforgettable spiritual moment.

LIVING HERITAGE

Kathmandu, Patan & Bhaktapur Durbar Squares

These medieval royal plazas showcase exquisite Newari architecture, temples, and palaces. Patan is famous for its art, Bhaktapur for its pottery and preserved medieval feel.

Flavors of Kathmandu: Newari Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes

  • Samay Baji — The ultimate Newari platter with beaten rice, choila (spiced meat), eggs, black soybeans, and more.
  • Chatamari — Nepali-style rice crepe, often called "Newari pizza."
  • Yomari — Sweet or savory dumplings filled with molasses and sesame, traditionally eaten during winter.
  • Juju Dhau — "King of Yogurts" from Bhaktapur — creamy, sweet, and legendary.
  • Bara & Choila — Lentil pancakes and spicy grilled buffalo meat.
  • Sapu Mhicha — Fried buffalo leaf tripe stuffed with bone marrow (for the adventurous!).

"Newari food is not just about taste — it's a celebration of community, seasons, and centuries-old traditions. Best enjoyed in a bhoye (feast) sitting on the floor with family and friends."

— Local food lovers in Kathmandu

Festivals in Kathmandu 2026

April 2026

Bisket Jatra & Nepali New Year

Massive chariot processions in Bhaktapur. Celebrates the start of Bikram Sambat 2083.

February 2026

Maha Shivaratri

Thousands gather at Pashupatinath Temple for all-night prayers and rituals dedicated to Lord Shiva.

September 2026

Indra Jatra

Kathmandu's grandest street festival with the Living Goddess Kumari procession.

Other highlights: Dashain (biggest festival), Tihar, Buddha Jayanti, and Holi.

Travel Tips for Kathmandu 2026

Best Time

March–May or October–November for clear Himalayan views.

Food Safety

Stick to bottled water and well-cooked street food in Thamel.

Getting Around

Use taxis, ride-sharing apps, or walk in Thamel and Durbar areas.

Respect

Dress modestly at temples. Remove shoes before entering sacred spaces.
