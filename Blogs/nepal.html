Welcome to the
Roof of the World

Home to the world's highest mountains, ancient temples, diverse cultures, and the warmest people on Earth.

Nepal is a land of breathtaking contrasts. From the towering peaks of the Himalayas (including Mount Everest) to the lush Terai plains, from ancient Hindu and Buddhist kingdoms to vibrant ethnic diversity — this small country packs an incredible amount of natural beauty, cultural richness, and spiritual depth.

With 8 of the world's 14 highest mountains above 8,000 meters, over 125 ethnic groups, and more than 120 languages, Nepal is one of the most culturally and geographically diverse nations on the planet.

The Majestic Himalayas

WORLD'S HIGHEST PEAKS

Mount Everest (Sagarmatha)

Standing at 8,848.86 meters, Everest is the crown jewel of Nepal. Known locally as Sagarmatha ("Forehead in the Sky"), it draws adventurers from around the world.

Popular Treks
  • • Everest Base Camp Trek
  • • Annapurna Circuit
  • • Langtang Valley
  • • Manaslu Circuit
Best Seasons

Spring (March–May) and Autumn (September–November) for clear skies and safer climbing conditions.

Rich Cultural Tapestry

HERITAGE

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Kathmandu Valley (7 sites), Lumbini (birthplace of Buddha), Chitwan National Park, and the sacred Sagarmatha National Park.

SPIRITUALITY

Birthplace of Buddha

Lumbini in southern Nepal is where Siddhartha Gautama (Lord Buddha) was born around 623 BC. A major pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.

DIVERSITY

125+ Ethnic Groups

From Sherpas and Gurungs in the mountains to Tharus and Maithilis in the Terai, Nepal celebrates incredible ethnic and linguistic diversity.

Wildlife & Biodiversity

Chitwan National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Asia’s best wildlife destinations. Home to the endangered One-horned Rhinoceros, Bengal Tiger, and over 500 species of birds.

  • 🦏 Greater One-horned Rhinoceros
  • 🐅 Royal Bengal Tiger
  • 🐊 Gharial Crocodile

"Nepal protects 23.39% of its land as national parks and conservation areas — one of the highest proportions in the world."

- Department of National Parks, Nepal

Vibrant Festivals of Nepal 2026

March–April 2026

Bisket Jatra & Nepali New Year

Grand chariot festivals and celebrations marking the start of Bikram Sambat 2083.

October–November 2026

Dashain & Tihar

Nepal’s biggest and most joyful festivals. Dashain celebrates victory of good over evil; Tihar honors animals and siblings.

May 2026

Buddha Jayanti

Celebration of Lord Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and passing — especially grand in Lumbini and Boudhanath.

Essential Travel Tips for Nepal 2026

Visa

On-arrival visa available at Tribhuvan International Airport for most nationalities.

Best Time to Visit

March–May (Spring) and September–November (Autumn) for trekking and mountain views.

People

"Namaste" is the universal greeting. Nepalese people are known for their genuine hospitality.

Cuisine

Try Dal Bhat (national dish), Momos, Newari Samay Baji, and Thukpa in the mountains.
